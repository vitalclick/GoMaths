# GoMaths — 5-minute demo script

For investor meetings, pilot-school pitches, and any high-stakes
walkthrough. Two audiences, mostly the same script, with the
divergences flagged below.

> **Setup (do this 30 min before)**
>
> - Have the backend, AI services, and Student app running locally.
> - `TUTOR_PROVIDER=anthropic` with a real key for live Maya replies.
>   If you can't, fall back to the mock — own it, don't hide it
>   (the conversation is still real, just deterministic).
> - Pre-create one demo student in Postgres so you don't burn 90 seconds
>   on registration. See [§ Pre-stage](#pre-stage).
> - Have the **wrong-maths recovery move** ready (§ "If Maya goofs").

## 30-second elevator (before you open the laptop)

> "GoMaths is an AI-powered maths app for Grade R–12 learners aligned
> to the South African CAPS curriculum. Today we're going to walk
> through what a Grade 9 learner does in five minutes: a lesson, a
> practice question, and a chat with Maya — the AI tutor. The
> distinguishing thing about Maya is she shows her working **and we
> verify the working before showing it to the student**. So you can
> trust the answer she gives."

## Scene 1 — Sign in (15 seconds)

Open the Student app on web (Chromebook-style; it's also iOS + Android).

```
URL: http://localhost:8081 (or the staging URL)
Email: demo@gomaths.co.za
Password: ********
```

**Talk track**: "Same app on every platform. School learners on
Chromebooks, learners at home on a parent's phone — same experience.
This is what we mean by mobile-first AND web-first."

## Scene 2 — The dashboard (15 seconds)

The signed-in dashboard shows: name + grade, "Browse topics", "Chat with Maya", "Scan an equation", "My progress".

**Talk track**: "Four things a learner does — pick a topic, ask a
question, scan a problem from a textbook, see their progress. That's
the entire surface area. Deliberately small for Grade 9."

## Scene 3 — A lesson (45 seconds)

Tap **Browse topics → Solving Linear Equations**.

The lesson renders as proper maths, with KaTeX equations like $2x + 5 = 13$.

**Talk track**: "These lessons are written by SACE-registered SA maths
teachers — not generated by AI. Every question's answer is verified by
a symbolic maths engine before it can be merged into the curriculum.
That CI check has caught wrong answers in authoring, and that's the
moat — _localised, verified, CAPS-aligned content_."

Scroll past one worked example so they see real maths render properly.
Don't read it aloud — it's about looking right, not being a maths
lesson.

## Scene 4 — Practice (60 seconds)

Tap **Practice this topic**. First question: `2*x + 5 = 13`.

Type `x = 4`. Tap **Check answer**.

**"Correct"** + worked solution appears + the "Verified by SymPy" line.

**Talk track**: "Two things happened: the learner answered, and the
backend ran the answer through a symbolic-maths checker. That's
'validated' — it's mathematical truth, not string-matching. If the
learner had written `x = 8/2` instead of `x = 4`, that would also be
correct, and we'd recognise it."

(Investor audience): "_This_ is why edtech math apps that just
string-compare against an expected answer can't scale. We solved that
on day one."

## Scene 5 — Chat with Maya (90 seconds — the centerpiece)

Tap **Chat with Maya**. Type:

> Why does x equal 4?

Maya streams a reply in. Below it: **"Maths verified"** with a green
dot (or "Verifying maths: x/y" if you catch it mid-stream).

**Talk track** (while streaming):

- "She's streaming her reply in real time — same UX as ChatGPT."
- "Notice the small green dot appearing below — that's the SymPy
  validator scoring her work as the maths claims stream in. If she
  said something wrong, the badge would not light up, and we wouldn't
  let the learner walk away thinking they'd been taught wrong maths."

When it finishes, point at the **"Maths verified"** line.

Optional follow-up: "Now try teaching me using a different example."
Lets you show multi-turn — the conversation persists, she remembers
the context.

(Investor audience): "This is the durable advantage. LLMs get maths
wrong all the time. We're not pretending they don't. We catch it."

## Scene 6 — Progress + close (45 seconds)

Tap back to dashboard → **My progress**.

Shows the topic with mastery score (66% after one correct + one
wrong, for example).

**Talk track**: "Phase 2 turns this into personalised recommendations
— Maya proactively suggesting what to practice next. Phase 3 turns it
into a parent-facing weekly digest. Today: it's clean, it's honest, it
works."

Close on the dashboard. **Don't tap any feature you don't have**.

## What NOT to show

- **The Tutor Marketplace** (Phase 1.5). It's documented but not built.
  If asked: "That's our second product. We unlock it once the core
  learning-outcome metric is proven in pilot."
- **The Parent app login**. Boots; nothing useful behind it yet. If
  asked: "It's the entry point. We light it up once Phase 1 ships."
- **Scan solver** if MathPix isn't wired. The mock OCR returns a
  canned equation; it'll feel obviously fake to a technical audience.
  If MathPix is real, **show it** — it's a wow moment.

## Expected questions + honest answers

**Q: Does the AI ever give wrong answers?**

> "Yes — every LLM does. That's why we validate the maths claims in
> every reply. When validation fails, the UI tells the learner the
> answer isn't fully verified. We don't pretend it's perfect; we make
> it observably honest."

**Q: How is this different from Photomath / Khan Academy / ChatGPT?**

> "Photomath solves; we teach. Khan is global; we're CAPS-aligned and
> in SA languages [Phase 2]. ChatGPT lies about maths confidently; we
> verify. The combination — local curriculum + verified AI tutor — is
> nobody else's product."

**Q: What happens if the LLM provider has an outage?**

> "We failover between Anthropic and OpenAI with an env-var change —
> ~30 seconds. If both are down, the app honestly tells the learner
> Maya's offline and continues to work for lessons + practice. Our
> runbook is in `runbooks/tutor-outage.md`."

**Q: What's your moat?**

> "Three things: localised CAPS-aligned curriculum content authored by
> SACE-registered teachers; symbolic-maths verification of every AI
> reply; and pilot relationships with [N] schools in [provinces]. None
> of those is buildable on a weekend."

**Q: When can you have my school in the pilot?**

(Pilot audience): "We're piloting with 4 schools starting <date>. Term
2 is full; we have 2 slots open for Term 3. Here's the MOU template."

(Investor audience): "We're piloting with 4 schools from Month 9 of
Phase 1. Each pilot school commits to a 12-week measurement window
with a matched control group."

**Q: What's the unit economics?**

(Investor only): "Phase 1 is pilot-free; we're measuring learning
outcomes, not revenue. Phase 2 turns on B2C subscriptions (~R 99/mo
benchmarked against PracticeMaths) and B2B school licences (~R 60
per seat per year). LTV/CAC modelling is in our deck — happy to
share."

## If Maya goofs (the most important contingency)

Sometimes the live demo gives a wrong answer the validator catches.
**This is good for you** — show it.

**Talk track**:

> "Look — see how the badge says 'Reply not fully verified'? Maya
> made a small mistake mid-reasoning, and our system caught it. The
> learner sees this label and knows to double-check with their teacher.
> No edtech demo I've seen does this. We built it specifically because
> AI gets maths wrong, and pretending otherwise is how you lose trust."

Then move on. Don't dwell.

## Pre-stage

Before the meeting:

```sh
# Create the demo student via the API (replace with your real backend URL):
curl -X POST http://localhost:4000/api/auth/register \
  -H 'content-type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "email": "demo@gomaths.co.za",
    "password": "<demo-password>",
    "displayName": "Aisha",
    "grade": 9
  }'

# Pre-warm one practice attempt so My Progress isn't empty:
# (sign in via the app first; then in the app, answer one question)

# Sanity-check the tutor is responsive:
curl -X POST http://localhost:4000/api/tutor/messages \
  -H "authorization: Bearer $(...)" \
  -H 'content-type: application/json' \
  -d '{"message":"hi maya","topicId":"g9.alg.linear-eq"}'
```

If the live tutor call takes >5 seconds, **either** pre-warm
Anthropic's cache by sending the same system prompt once before the
meeting, **or** switch `TUTOR_PROVIDER=mock` and tell the audience
you're using the mock provider for reliability.

## Calendar follow-up

Within 24 hours: a short email with three things:

1. One sentence recap of what they saw working.
2. One sentence of what's coming next (Phase 2 personalisation OR Tutor Marketplace, depending on which they cared about).
3. One specific ask — sign the pilot MOU / introduce one investor in
   their network / 30-min follow-up with their CTO.

Don't write four asks. Pick the one.
