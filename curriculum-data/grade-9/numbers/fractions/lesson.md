# Operations with Fractions

A fraction $\dfrac{a}{b}$ has a **numerator** $a$ (top) and **denominator**
$b$ (bottom).

## Simplifying

Divide top and bottom by a common factor: $\dfrac{5}{10} = \dfrac{1}{2}$.

## Adding and subtracting

You need a **common denominator** first.

$$\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{5}{6}$$

When the denominators already match, just add or subtract the numerators:
$\dfrac{3}{4} - \dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{2}{4} = \dfrac{1}{2}$.

> **Never** add the denominators. $\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3} \neq \frac{2}{5}$.

## Multiplying

Multiply straight across, then simplify:

$$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$$

## Dividing

**Multiply by the reciprocal** (flip the second fraction):

$$\frac{2}{5} \div \frac{4}{5} = \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{5}{4} = \frac{10}{20} = \frac{1}{2}$$

## Always simplify your final answer to lowest terms.
