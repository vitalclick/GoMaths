# Operations with Integers

**Integers** are the whole numbers and their negatives:
$\dots, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \dots$

## Adding and subtracting

Think of a number line. Adding moves **right**, subtracting moves **left**.

- $-5 + 8 = 3$ (start at $-5$, move $8$ right)
- $2 - 9 = -7$ (start at $2$, move $9$ left)

**Subtracting a negative** is the same as adding:

$$-7 - (-2) = -7 + 2 = -5$$

## Multiplying and dividing — the sign rules

| Signs        | Result   |
| ------------ | -------- |
| $+ \times +$ | positive |
| $- \times -$ | positive |
| $+ \times -$ | negative |
| $- \times +$ | negative |

Same rules apply to division. Examples:

- $-3 \times -4 = 12$ (two negatives → positive)
- $-12 \div 4 = -3$ (different signs → negative)

## The big trap

**Two negatives make a positive only for × and ÷.** For subtraction,
$-7 - (-2)$ becomes $-7 + 2$, not $-7 - 2$.
