# Percentages

A **percentage** is a fraction out of 100. "Per cent" literally means
"per hundred", so $25\% = \dfrac{25}{100} = 0.25$.

## Finding a percentage of a quantity

Convert the percentage to a decimal, then multiply.

- $5\%$ of $80 = 0.05 \times 80 = 4$
- $20\%$ of $150 = 0.2 \times 150 = 30$
- $15\%$ of $200 = 0.15 \times 200 = 30$

> Watch the decimal: $5\% = 0.05$, not $0.5$.

## Expressing one quantity as a percentage of another

Divide, then multiply by 100.

$$\frac{45}{180} \times 100 = 25 \quad\Rightarrow\quad 45 \text{ is } 25\% \text{ of } 180$$

## Converting between forms

| Fraction      | Decimal | Percentage |
| ------------- | ------- | ---------- |
| $\frac{3}{4}$ | $0.75$  | $75\%$     |
| $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0.5$   | $50\%$     |
| $\frac{1}{4}$ | $0.25$  | $25\%$     |

To go **fraction → percentage**, multiply by 100:
$\frac{3}{4} \times 100 = 75\%$.
