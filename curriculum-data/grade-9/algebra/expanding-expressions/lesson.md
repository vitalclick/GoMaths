# Expanding Algebraic Expressions

**Expanding** (or "removing brackets") means rewriting a product as a sum
of terms. It is the reverse of factorising.

## 1. The distributive law

Multiply every term inside the bracket by the term outside:

$$a(b + c) = ab + ac$$

**Example:** $2(3x - 5) = 6x - 10$.

## 2. Multiplying two binomials

Multiply each term in the first bracket by each term in the second. A handy
reminder is **FOIL** — First, Outer, Inner, Last:

$$(x + 2)(x + 3) = x^2 + 3x + 2x + 6 = x^2 + 5x + 6$$

## 3. Two patterns worth memorising

**Difference of squares:**

$$(a - b)(a + b) = a^2 - b^2$$

so $(x - 4)(x + 4) = x^2 - 16$ — the middle terms cancel.

**Perfect square:**

$$(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$$

so $(x + 5)^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25$. The most common mistake is forgetting the
middle term $2ab$.

## Tip

Always **collect like terms** at the end, and remember that a coefficient
in front of a bracket (like the $2$ in $2x$) multiplies through too.
