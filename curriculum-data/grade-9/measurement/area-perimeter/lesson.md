# Area and Perimeter

**Perimeter** is the distance around a shape. **Area** is the space it
covers (measured in square units).

## Rectangles and squares

- **Perimeter of a rectangle** $= 2(l + b)$, e.g. $2(7 + 3) = 20$.
- **Area of a rectangle** $= l \times b$, e.g. $8 \times 6 = 48$.
- **Area of a square** $= s^2$, e.g. $9^2 = 81$.

## Triangles

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$$

For example $\tfrac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 4 = 20$. The height must be the
**perpendicular** height, not a slanted side.

## Circles

$$\text{Area} = \pi r^2$$

With radius $5$: $\pi \times 5^2 = 25\pi$. Leave answers **in terms of
$\pi$** unless asked to round.

> Watch the units: perimeter is in units, area is in **square** units.
> The most common mistake is multiplying when you should add (or vice
> versa) — perimeter adds the sides, area multiplies them.
